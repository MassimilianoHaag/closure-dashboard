## Sync rhythm

- **Daily check-in:** 15 minutes, same time every day. Voice or video. Two questions: *what did you do, what's blocking you.*
- **Mid-week deep sync:** 60–90 minutes, midweek. Review the prototype state, discuss iteration log entries, decide pivots if needed.
- **Async by default the rest of the time.** Use the Working Log to hand off.

If the daily check-in becomes a chore, shorten it, don't skip it. The cost of skipping is a day of misalignment.

## Blocker protocol

If you're stuck for more than 30 minutes, ping the other person. Do not silently push through. The week is too short.

If both of you are stuck, write it as a question in the relevant stage folder, sleep on it, decide the next morning. Most "stuck" feels worse than it is.

---

## Parallel work without collision

We have two streams running side by side:

- **Stream A — Product process** (Design Thinking stages 1–4, plus the prototype builds)
- **Stream B — Presentation** (slides, demo script, Q&A prep)

**Rule of thumb:** Stream B doesn't really kick off until the last 2–3 days. Until then, both of us are in Stream A, but in different stage folders. We coordinate via the Working Log so we're not editing the same file.

If we ever find ourselves both in the same file at the same time: stop, talk for 60 seconds, decide who has the pencil.

## Non-negotiables (copied from Read_Me)

1. Update the Working Log as we iterate. Use `/log` — two entry types:
   - **[LOG]** — end of session. What you did, what's next. Most entries are this.
   - **[CHANGE]** — a decision that has lasting effect on the design or build. What changed, why, impact.
2. Test at every prototype pass, not just at the end.
3. Every design decision evaluated against "does this sharpen the closing ritual or dilute it?"
4. The brief's "what to avoid" list stays on the wall.

---

## What we do if we disagree

We disagree often, on purpose. That's good design work. But when it stalls:

1. State the disagreement in one sentence each, in writing (in the relevant folder).
2. Identify what evidence would resolve it — a corridor test, a small experiment, a reference.
3. Run the test, look at the evidence, decide.
4. If no evidence is available, the primary owner of that stage decides. Move on.

We don't relitigate decisions once made unless new evidence surfaces. Log them as a [CHANGE] entry if it helps.

---

## Mood check (don't skip this)

The week will get hard. Burnout makes both of us stupid. If either of us is fried:

- Say so. "I'm fried" is a complete sentence.
- Step away for a few hours. The other carries.
- Come back when you can think again.

The product is better made by two rested people than two exhausted ones.
