## The product, in two sentences

Closure is a closing ritual for finished work. Creative professionals (designers, architects) speak for 60 seconds the moment they ship a project, and the product turns that capture into a documented case study that lives in a private gallery — eventually publishable as a portfolio without the user ever "working on their portfolio."

## Working conventions

**File naming:** `YYYY-MM-DD_Topic_Author.md` for dated artifacts. Underscores, not spaces. Topic_vN for design file versions.

**Ownership tag:** Every working document has `Owner: Max` or `Owner: Guz` or `Owner: Both` at the top. When ownership changes, update it.

**Status tag (optional, useful):**
- `Status: Draft` — work in progress, don't quote yet
- `Status: For Review` — ready for the other person
- `Status: Final` — locked, feeds the next stage

**Daily ritual:** Before you close your laptop, add two lines to `00_Project/00_Working_Log.md`. The next person reads that first.

**Versioning:** For Markdown docs, edit in place — OneDrive's history covers us. For Figma files, save dated snapshots to the relevant Prototype pass folder at the end of each pass.

**Don't touch `07_Presentation/` until the final 2–3 days.** It gets assembled from the artifacts in the other folders. We don't build it from scratch.

## The non-negotiables

These are the things we agreed not to drift on, no matter how busy the week gets:

1. **Update the Working Log as we iterate.** Use `/log` — [CHANGE] entries for design decisions (what changed, why, impact), [LOG] entries at the end of each session. Skipping this means reconstructing the iteration journey at the end, which is much worse than maintaining it.

2. **Test at every prototype pass, not just at the end.** Three corridor tests across the week. Each round feeds the next prototype pass.

3. **The mechanism is "a closing ritual for finished work."** Every design decision is evaluated against this. If it sharpens the ritual, keep it. If it dilutes it, cut it.

4. **The brief's "what to avoid" list is on the wall, metaphorically.** No generic to-do-list-with-AI. The first action stays clear. The interface stays simple. The scope stays focused.
