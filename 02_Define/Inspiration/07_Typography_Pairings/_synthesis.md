# Typography Pairings — Synthesis
**Owner:** Both
**Status:** Draft — decision layer on top of the three CSV tables

---

## Section 1 — What's in each CSV

**1 — Modern Literary Essay:** Serif headline (Piazzolla/Ogg) paired with humanist sans body (Pangea/Inter). Warm, high-readability system built for extended reading. The 18px/1.65 body is the most legible of the three at case-study length. Literary magazine register.

**2 — Avant-Garde Swiss Grid:** All-sans system built from a single family (Founders Grotesk) with extreme weight contrast — 800 Black headline, 400 Regular body. GT America Mono for captions. Graphic design studio energy, editorial but aggressive. Zero warmth, maximum clarity.

**3 — Architectural Fine Art Spread:** Delicate light-weight serif throughout (Cormorant Garamond 300–400) paired with Helvetica Neue Light body. Fine-art catalog register — airy, precious, low contrast. Architecturally minded but fragile at small sizes.

---

## Section 2 — Common patterns

- **Serif headline + sans body** appears in two of three systems (#1 and #3); it's the dominant convention across the three directions.
- **Generous body line-height:** all three sit between 1.4–1.7. Nobody goes tight. Closure's case study output should inherit this.
- **Caption/meta lives at 11–13px** with a distinct treatment — monospace, tight tracking, or medium weight on small text — to differentiate utility text from reading text.
- **Two-level header hierarchy only** (H1 + H2). No H3 in any table. Closure probably doesn't need one either.
- **Weight contrast is always intentional:** either within-family (CSV 2: 800 vs 400) or cross-category (CSV 1: serif bold vs sans regular).

---

## Section 3 — Direction-specific recommendations

**Intellectual Sanctuary (editorial/academic):** CSV 1 — Piazzolla + Inter. Most directly editorial. Warm, readable, anchored in the essay tradition. The serif headline earns the "this is worth reading" signal that the case study output needs.

**Espresso & Linen (warm grown-up):** CSV 1 again — Piazzolla + Pangea/Inter. It's the warmest pairing in the set. CSV 3 reads too cold and precious; CSV 2 reads too stark.

**Wabi-Sabi Mineral (quiet neutral):** CSV 3 — Cormorant Garamond + Helvetica Neue Light. The lightness and restraint match the register. Best paired with generous whitespace to stop it reading as fragile.

---

## Section 4 — Defaults

If we had to ship today: **Piazzolla** (headline) + **Inter** (body). Both are on Google Fonts, well-hinted, proven in editorial UI, and the pairing holds across all three aesthetic directions without committing hard to any one.
