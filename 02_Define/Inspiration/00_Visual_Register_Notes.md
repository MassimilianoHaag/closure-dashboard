## Section 1 — Aesthetic target

Closure activates in a private moment — the user has just finished something and is still close to it. The interface should feel like a trusted notebook: intimate enough that speaking into it doesn't feel performative, professional enough that the output feels worth keeping. The register is grown-up-not-precious: it earns its quietness through restraint, not minimalism as a pose — whitespace that holds the content, not whitespace that signals effort.

---

## Section 2 — What we are NOT

- **Corporate SaaS dashboards** — no data-dense layouts, no nav rails with 12 items, no "workspace" energy
- **Wellness app pastels** — no sage green, no soft peach, no rounded-corner-everything, no "breathe" vibes
- **Notion-style productivity density** — we are not a tool people live in all day; we're a 60-second ritual
- **Hackathon-typical AI purple/gradient** — no neon accents, no glowing orbs, no "powered by AI" aesthetics
- **Celebration / confetti UI** — this is a closing, not a celebration; restraint over dopamine
- **Empty consumer app cheerfulness** — no illustrated characters, no "you're doing great!" micro-copy

---

## Section 3 — Synthesis

**Split-register approach (locked).** Capture / recording / AI reflection screens use a dark surface. Gallery / case study output / publish screens use parchment cream. The split is intentional: you enter a quiet, private space to do the work, and emerge into a warm, curated space where the work lives.

**Color tokens:**
- `#EFEBE4` Parchment cream — light register background
- `#3A3D40` Deep charcoal — light register text; dark register background variant
- `#3E322A` Espresso brown — accent, both registers, applied per Linear rule (reserved for highest-priority signal only)
- Secondary: soft stone grays and warm off-whites for supporting elements; light cream for text in the dark register

**Typography:**
- **Piazzolla** (Google Fonts, variable serif) — headlines and emotionally weighted moments; earns scale on output screens; used sparingly in the capture register
- **Inter** (Google Fonts, variable sans) — body, UI chrome, primary type in the capture register

**Design principles:**
- Linear rule: espresso reserved for the single highest-priority element per screen
- Empty states: poetic single CTA, no explanation ("Your first capture lives here," not "Add your first project")
- Never put metrics, stats, or marketing-template elements near output content (negative ref: Format portfolio template, folder 04)
