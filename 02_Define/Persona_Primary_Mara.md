# Persona — Mara Santos
**Owner:** Max
**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Source:** Empathize stage synthesis — survey n=5, secondary research (11 sources)
**Feeds:** Problem statement, HMW questions, prototype trigger mechanic

---

## Who she is

Product designer, 29, four years at a mid-size digital agency. She ships 2–4 projects a year and has genuine craft. Her Figma files are clean. Her portfolio hasn't been touched in eighteen months.

---

## Tagline

*"I do good work. I just can't write about it."*

---

## Her experience level with Closure

First-time user. Comfortable with AI for image generation and copy drafts, but skeptical of AI quality in professional contexts. She'll try something once if the pitch is credible.

---

## When she uses Closure

Just finished a three-month brand and UX project. The deliverable went out Thursday. By Friday morning the guilt surfaced — she opens Closure not because she planned to, but because the threshold was low enough.

---

## Her goals

A portfolio that reflects where she actually is in her career — not where she was two years ago. She wants the documentation to feel proportionate to the work: shipping took months; this shouldn't feel like a second job.

---

## What gets in the way

**Writing** is the primary blocker. She can describe her work fluently in a meeting or a Slack thread — the moment she opens a blank document, something stiffens.

**Momentum loss** is the second. By the time she'd sit down properly, the project is over emotionally. Re-entering that headspace feels like starting a second project on something she already finished.

**The blank page** is the third. She knows the story. She doesn't know where to start, what detail level is right, which decisions are worth surfacing.

---

## Her concerns about Closure

*"Will the output actually be good?"* She's been burned by AI-generated text that sounds generic and stripped of her voice. Her bar is not "perfect draft" — it's "something I can actually edit and ship." She also doesn't want to be flattered; she wants the harder follow-up question.

---

## Representative quote

*"I wanted to move on to the next thing. Also, I didn't know where to start and how to document the project in an effective way."*
— Survey respondent R4, UX/product designer, 2–5 years experience

---

## What success looks like for Mara

She speaks for sixty seconds, answers one follow-up question, edits the draft twice, and publishes it. Four months later her portfolio has six updated case studies. She did not become a writer. She did not have to.

---

## What this persona is based on

Writing-as-bottleneck is fully triangulated (primary + secondary). Emotional avoidance and momentum-loss confirmed in primary data (R1, R2, R4). Blank-page blocker directly quoted from R4. Perfectionism excluded — strong in secondary research but silent in primary; not load-bearing here.
