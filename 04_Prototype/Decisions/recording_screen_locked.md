# Recording Screen — Locked Decision
**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Variant chosen:** B — Spoken words as text
**Source PNG:** `05_Prototype/Pass_1_LowFi/Variants/recording/variant_b.png`

## Decision

Variant B is the Pass 1 direction for the Recording screen. The mechanic — live transcription appearing word by word in Piazzolla italic, cut off mid-sentence — is readable without animation, which means it communicates correctly as a static wireframe. It is the only variant where the product's emotional premise (the app listening hard enough to write down your exact words) is enacted by the design itself rather than implied. The visual register is fully compliant: espresso used once as the stop control, Piazzolla and Inter as specified, no accent drift.

---

## What A and C taught us

**Variant A** used espresso as a large circular container around the waveform — a structural element, not an accent. This breaks the Linear rule and shifts the feeling from intimate to surveilled. Lesson captured in `_global_constraints.md`: no decorative rings or frames; accents are text-weight or single-element, never structural containers.

**Variant C** demonstrated a general lo-fi limitation: a static design cannot distinguish "actively recording" from "tap to begin" when the only differentiating element is animation. Any screen whose primary state depends on motion needs an additional static signal. Note carried forward.
